# Spark Terms and AI Screening Disclosure

Effective date: June 6, 2026  
Version: 2026-06-06-v2  
Entities: WMBE Payrolling Inc. dba TCWGlobal and Cloud Motion Technologies

This document is the source copy for Spark's public terms and AI screening
disclosure pages. It is written for candidate-facing use and should be reviewed
by counsel before final publication as a binding legal policy.

## Overview

Spark is a candidate intake and recruiter review workflow used for job
opportunities published by StaffingNation. Spark may use automated workflow
tools, AI-assisted transcript review, AI-assisted summarization, and secure
cloud infrastructure to help recruiters review job-related application
materials. Spark does not make final hiring, firing, termination, or
compensation decisions without human review.

## AI-assisted screening

When a candidate chooses the standard Spark workflow, Spark may process
candidate-provided profile information, written responses, interview answers,
device signals, and permission-based media or location data to support:

- job-related qualification review;
- recruiter summaries and workflow routing;
- interview question and answer organization;
- fraud, identity, and duplicate-submission review;
- regional work-eligibility and jurisdiction checks where relevant to the job.

AI-assisted outputs are used as recruiter support tools. Human recruiters and
hiring managers remain responsible for advancement and hiring decisions.

## Device permissions and collected data

Spark may ask for permission to access device capabilities only when needed for
the workflow:

- Microphone: records spoken answers and supports transcript generation.
- Camera: records video interview answers and supports integrity review.
- Browser geolocation: captures location signals when relevant to jurisdiction,
  workplace proximity, fraud, or compliance review.
- Browser and device metadata: records limited technical signals such as user
  agent, language, time zone, platform, and screen dimensions for security and
  troubleshooting.

Spark also collects ordinary application data such as name, email, phone,
location provided by the candidate, availability, job selection, application
status, recruiter notes, interview content, and communication preferences.

## California notice

California candidates may have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act,
as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, and under California employment
and civil rights rules governing automated-decision systems. Spark provides a
Notice at Collection before collecting candidate data and offers a manual review
pathway for candidates who do not want to use the standard AI-assisted screening
workflow.

Spark does not sell candidate personal information. Spark is designed so that
AI-assisted tools do not make final binding employment decisions. Candidates
with questions or legally required accommodation needs may contact
compliance@tcwglobal.com.

## Questions, accommodation, and human review

The only manual review request channel is email. Candidates who want to request
manual review must email compliance@tcwglobal.com and attach a current resume.
Compliance may forward the request and resume to recruiting for awareness or
review. Spark does not promise a response, interview, video review, status
update, or advancement from that request.

Applications may still be declined based on objective, job-related
requirements, incomplete information, availability, location, legal
requirements, or other lawful criteria. Spark should not reject a candidate
merely because the candidate made a legally protected accommodation or privacy
request.

## Accommodations

Candidates who need an accommodation because of disability, religious practice,
accessibility needs, device constraints, or another protected reason may request
assistance by contacting compliance@tcwglobal.com.

## Retention and deletion

Spark retains candidate information only as long as needed for recruitment,
compliance, security, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, and legal
recordkeeping. Where permitted by law, Spark may retain a limited application
record after deleting or de-identifying sensitive interview materials. This
limited record may help prevent duplicate, abusive, or rapid reapplications for
the same role and may support employment recordkeeping obligations.

Candidate video, audio, transcript, resume, and location information should not
be used to train public AI models.

## International applicants

Where privacy laws outside the United States apply, candidates may have
additional rights, including rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object
to, or receive a copy of personal data, and rights related to automated
decision-making or profiling. Spark should honor those rights where applicable.
